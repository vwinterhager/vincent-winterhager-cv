I am an economist and policy officer working at the intersection of European fiscal policy, political economy, and applied econometrics — currently in the Chief Economist Unit of DG BUDG, and teaching the political economy of the EU budget at the Hertie School.

At the European Commission I work in the Chief Economist team of DG BUDG, strengthening the econometric evidence base on the EU budget together with the Joint Research Centre to inform decision-makers ahead of the next Multiannual Financial Framework (MFF). Previously, in the Ukraine Directorate of DG ENEST, I coordinated the G7+ Energy Group for Ukraine, led economic analysis on energy-sector state-owned enterprises, the impact of CBAM on Ukraine's industry, and public debt and inflation, and was responsible for the energy and climate reforms of the Ukraine Facility as well as the Rapid Damage and Needs Assessment with the World Bank. Before that, I worked on the Recovery and Resilience Facility (RRF) in the Secretariat General, handling the country plans of Estonia and Belgium and coordinating REPowerEU negotiations.

Alongside the Commission, I teach a graduate seminar at the Hertie School on the political economy of common debt in the EU, the future of the MFF, and diverging productivity regimes across the Union — co-taught with Johannes Lindner of the Jacques Delors Centre. I hold a dual master's degree from the London School of Economics (MPA) and the Hertie School (MPP), and I am an alumnus and fellow of the Friedrich Ebert Foundation.
